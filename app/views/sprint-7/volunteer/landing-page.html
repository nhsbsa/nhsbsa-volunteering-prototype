There are many ways you can help. Find out what the different roles are and what they entail.

  • Community support

    Become an active member of your community and make a positive impact on the lives of your neighbours.

  • Patient-facing companionship and support

    Enhance the quality and comfort of patients from the moment they walk through the door and through their stay.

  • Leisure activities

    Lift patients' spirit and enhance their experience through a range of creative activities.

  • Opportunties for young volunteers (16-18)

    A range of roles tailored to young people (16 to 18) wishing to kick start their volunteering.

  • Volunteer admin support

    Assist staff with general clerical tasks to improve volunteering activities.

  • Patient participation and experience

    Take part in research and engagement activities to ensure patients' voice informs real changes for NHS patients and staff.

  • Charity and fundraising

    Join the family of allies that help support and funding providing services to our patients, visitors, and staff.

  • Remote opportunities

    Continue making a difference to your community from a distance. You may require a phone or a computer and some basic tech skills.

What it is like to be an NHS volunteer

As an NHS volunteer, you get to make a positive contribution to the experience of patients and their families. 

There are many reasons why you may decide to volunteer within the NHS volunteer. 

You may find it rewarding to do something for the benefit of your local community. It might give you a sense of accomplishment and a positive outlook on life. You will be doing something worthwhile and meaningful to you. 

Volunteering can help you make a decision on your future goals. Whether you are studying or reconsidering your career, volunteering gives you the skills, experience and confidence you need. 

Whatever your motivation, you will get support from a dedicated team who welcomes volunteers from all walks of life and backgrounds.
