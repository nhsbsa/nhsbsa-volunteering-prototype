Exploring volunteering opportunities

There is a wide range of unpaid opportunities that you could help with.

Some can be a one-off. Others can last longer.

Most of the activities will be in person. There are some that you will be able to carry out from home too.

The types of activities, time commitment and training will depend on the needs of your trust.

There could be other factors to consider when choosing what activities you can get involved with.

If you have any concerns, discuss with the volunteering service manager at your chosen NHS trust what options are available to you.

Here are some of the most common activities:

Front of house

  • Information desk support
  • Meeting and greeting
  • Refreshment or library trolley services
  • Hospital shops, cafes or libraries
  • Read more about these roles

Patient and therapy support

  • Ambulance service volunteer
  • Befriender, visitor or companion for patients
  • Hospital/virtual research volunteer
  • Mealtimes volunteer
  • Patient transport and other driving support services
  • Peer support and other roles using lived experience
  • Pets or animals therapy
  • Providing support to people with dementia
  • Supporting clinical service delivery
  • Read more about these roles

Recreational activities

  • Gardening
  • Hospital radio and live entertainment
  • Patient entertainment activities and exercise
  • Read more about these roles

Admin and volunteering support

  • Admin or clerical support
  • Patient experience, surveys, or feedback collection
  • Read more about these roles
