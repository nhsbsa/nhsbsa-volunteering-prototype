Walk and talk Volunteer

Pennine Care NHS Trust

The closing date is 25 October 2022

Apply for this role Save for later

Role summary

This is a rewarding volunteering opportunity within our mental health services for people with memory problems or dementia and their carers. You will support our therapy team with the running to maintain and improve their well-being.

This role will suits someone friendly, approachable and enthusiastic. Whether you want to gain experience, improve your career prospects or just want to give some of your time to help others, that could be for you!

Main duties of the role

  • To encourage and support service users to participate in this therapeutic and social activity
  • To meet and greet service users as they arrive
  • To encourage people to participate and help them to feel good about themselves
  • To direct walkers along the designated route

About us

At XX, we have a team of brilliant, active and highly valued team of volunteers who give their time to make a difference for patients and medical staff every week.

Our volunteers have non-clinical roles that make a positive impact and improve the experience of patients using the hospital.

We have a dedicated team of staff who supports our volunteers from the start and provides comprehensive induction and on-going training opportunities. We can ensure you feel welcome and part of the team.

We welcome applications from all sections of the community and are keen to diversify our volunteer workforce.

Person specification

Qualifications and training

No qualifications or previous training are required.

You will undertake a training induction as well as on-going training which is essential to the role.

Experience, knowledge and skills

  • Good verbal communication skills
  • Reliability and good time keeping
  • Ability to effectively engage with those using the service
  • An enjoyment of the outdoors
  • No previous experience required
